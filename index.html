Creating Sustainable Living for African Farmers

We help farmers mitigate changing weather and water scarcity to promote food security while creating renewable energy through AI-powered agro-voltaic technology.

The Problem

Agriculture is the backbone of the African economy and most African homes. However, according to UNCTAD, more than 794.7 million people in Africa experience food insecurity. Challenges with access to affordable fertilizer, and fuel, depopulation of rural Africa, and drought cause this.

With the right support and more rural development, we can create sustainable farming that ensures our farmers harvest all year round and build sustainable lives for themselves.

Our Solution

Our innovation is affordable AI-powered Agrovoltaics. The technology combines voltaics (energy generation) and agriculture. The solar panels generate electricity while shading the crops and protecting the soil from loss of moisture.

  • Generates Renewable Energy
  • Improved Farming Practices
  • Sustainability

Why Farmers Need Our Technology

Generates Renewable Energy

The solar panels create energy for the farmers to use in their homesteads. Taswira Agrovoltaics plans to in the future collect and redistribute the energy generated across the country therefore creating additional income for the farmers.

Improved Farming Practices

The solar panels create shade from sunlight which is beneficial for many vegetables. It also helps the soil retain more moisture which helps mitigate drought and increases food production.

Sustainability

Agrovoltaics will generate green energy for homes in rural settings while creating sustainable farming and income for farmers.

Our Story

As a family, we have always had a farm. After our mother passed on and our father lost his job, the small kitchen garden ensured we did not sleep hungry. We grew leafy vegetables, onions, carrots, and other essentials. We can still remember working on it over the weekends with our father.

We thought we had seen it all until years later when our father retired to Laikipia County. This semi-arid area has extremely fertile soil. However, its people suffer from food insecurity due to drought. The children here do their homework with kerosene lamps exposing themselves to risks like fume inhalation and fires. This has caused more and more young people to move to the cities in search of better lives, further impoverishing the area.

In 2023 during drought, David visited Laikipia and was shocked to see the dire need for a solution. Livestock like cows were dropping dead due to hunger. Children went to school hungry, the land was completely bare. He needed to find a solution, and he did: Agrovoltaics. This marked the birth of Taswira Agrovoltaics.
